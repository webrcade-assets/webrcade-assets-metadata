Bermuda Square is a single player, keyboard controlled, maths/logic game provided by the XOR Corporation.The player is presented with twenty-four squares ranging in size from 1x1 to 24x24 and the object of the game was to fit as many squares as possible into a 70x70 grid. There is no pictorial representation of the grid with or without the squares inserted, the positions are shown by the co-ordinates of the top left corner and the position depends upon the order in which they are placedThe game was a contest. It was released in 1987 and closed on March 1, 1989, players submitted their solutions to the puzzle on an entry form found in their catalogue.
