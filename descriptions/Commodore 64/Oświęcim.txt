It is a turn-based strategy game based on decision-making, where our task is to play the role of Rudolf Höss and supervise the construction of the title concentration camp. The currency in the game is German marks, which the player can spend on the construction of barracks, crematoria or gas chambers, or on the recruitment of prisoners. At the end of the turn, the player determines how much food the workers will receive. For too many dead prisoners and the need to bury corpses in the forest, high fines must be paid. The game contains numerous random events. The game includes numerous random events. The game is unwinnable - it ends with a slightly alternative version of history - the camp is liberated on January 27, 1945, and Rudolf Höss is hanged for his crimes without waiting for the Nuremberg trials.
